Mental Health Prediction
Stress Prediction
Stress is the body’s natural response to challenges or demands. While some stress can be motivating, excessive or ongoing stress can negatively affect both mental and physical health. Students commonly experience stress due to academic pressure, finances, and social expectations.
Common symptoms include:
  • Feeling overwhelmed or out of control
  • Difficulty focusing or sleeping
  • Frequent headaches or fatigue
  • Mood swings or emotional outbursts
  • Reduced academic performance
Depression Prediction
Depression is a mood disorder marked by persistent sadness and a loss of interest in daily activities. It can significantly interfere with everyday life and academic performance if left unaddressed.
Common symptoms include:
  • Loss of motivation and energy
  • Feelings of worthlessness or hopelessness
  • Changes in appetite and sleep
  • Trouble concentrating
  • Thoughts of self-harm or suicide
Anxiety Prediction
Anxiety is characterized by ongoing and excessive worry, nervousness, or fear, even when there is little or no reason for concern. Persistent anxiety can interfere with concentration, decision-making, and sleep, impacting students’ academic and personal lives.
Common symptoms include:
  • Feeling on edge or restless
  • Uncontrollable worry
  • Trouble relaxing
  • Irritability
  • Physical symptoms like a racing heart or shortness of breath
